Unequal Burden: A Mapping Tool for Environmental Justice in Cancer Alley

In GEOG 575: Interactive Cartography & Geovisualization, our final project involved creating an interactive map to help a community leader advocate for understanding the cost of the relocation of harmed marginalized populations in Louisiana's Cancer Alley. Using the D3.js library, I built a dynamic, data-driven map that integrates cancer rates, air quality, refinery locations, and demographic data to clearly visualizes the disproportionate health impacts of industrial pollution. Through collaboration and user-centered design, we built a tool that empowers advocacy by showing the correlation between pollution, health outcomes, and housing costs, ultimately helping to secure funding for safer relocation options for at-risk communities.
