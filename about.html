Hi, I'm Leah Bates

I'm currently pursuing my Master’s in Cartography & GIS at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. Before that, I earned my BS in Environmental Science (with distinction), also at UW-Madison, which deeply shaped how I think about the intersections of nature and people.

Professionally I contributed to large-scale geospatial projects using orthophotography, LiDAR editing, and image enhancement that met USGS standards. I also bring creative skills from tools like Illustrator, Mapbox Studio, ArcGIS, and Photoshop to aid in clarity, style, and impact for all my maps.

I’m especially drawn to projects where environmental and social issues come together. Maps can show how things like pollution, access to resources, or health outcomes aren’t always distributed equally. Geography has a powerful way of highlighting those kinds of patterns, and that’s a big part of why I love making maps. I want to keep building work that brings these issues into focus and helps people see the bigger picture.
